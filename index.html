Sanskrit Verse:

समानी व आकूति: समाना हृदयानि वः |
समानमस्तु वो मनो यथा वः सुसहासति ||

Transliteration:

Samānī vā ākūtiḥ samānā hṛdayāni vaḥ |
Samānam astu vo mano yathā vaḥ susahāsati ||

DeSandhi Version:

समानी व आकूतिः समाना हृदयानि व: |
समानम् अस्तु व: मनः यथा व: सु-सह-आसति ||

Verse Source:

Rigveda

English Translation:

May your intentions be united, may your hearts be identical, may your minds be in agreement so that together you may perform your duties well.

Poetic Translation

In unity we weave our thoughts, a tapestry divine,
Where intents like streams converge, in purpose they align. Hearts resonate in chorus, a symphony of souls,
With each beat a promise, in the cosmic roles.

Mind to mind in concord, our visions share one sight,
In the sacred space between us, our spirits take their flight. Together we rise, with well-being as our guide,
In the dance of existence, side by side.

Grammatical Analysis:

  • “समानी” (samānī), “समाना” (samānā), and “समानम्” (samānam) are adjectives agreeing in case, number, and gender with the nouns they qualify: “आकूतिः” (ākūtiḥ), “हृदयानि” (hṛdayāni), and “मनः” (manaḥ) respectively.
  • “वः” (vaḥ) is a second-person plural pronoun used here as a suffix for possession or relation to the collective group addressed.
  • The imperative “अस्तु” (astu) expresses a wish or benediction.
  • The verse employs the plural form to indicate

a collective action or state of being, underscoring the communal aspect.

  • The compound “सुसहासति” (susahāsati) is formed from “सु” (su - well) + “सह” (sah - together) + “आसति” (āsati - sit/present), which metaphorically means to perform duties well together.

Cultural or Philosophical Significance:

The verse from the Rigveda emphasizes the importance of unity and harmony within a group. It acknowledges that collective success is dependent on the alignment of intent, emotions, and thoughts among individuals. This not only reflects the Vedic emphasis on societal harmony but also relates to the broader Indic concept of “Sangha,” where a community’s strength is seen as a function of its unity. The verse advocates for a synergy of purpose and effort, which is a timeless principle relevant to both spiritual and secular endeavors.

The Symphony of Syama


In the ancient village of Syama, nestled between the whispering forests and the serene river Sarayu, the villagers lived in discord. Each villager pursued their own goals in isolation, their hearts closed to the rhythms of their neighbors. The fields lay barren, the riverbanks were silent, and the forests echoed with the solitude of their toil.

One evening, as the sun dipped below the horizon, painting the sky with hues of orange and purple, a traveler named Aarav arrived. He carried with him nothing but an old flute and a heart full of melodies. As night fell, Aarav sat under the ancient Banyan tree at the village’s heart and began to play a tune that seemed to whisper of unity and harmony.

The villagers, drawn by the haunting melody, found themselves gathered around Aarav, their differences forgotten. They listened, enraptured, as his music wove their individual desires into a single thread of hope and community.

Moved by the music, the villagers began to speak of their dreams, not as solitary ambitions, but as visions they could achieve together. The farmers spoke of fields that could flourish if they shared the labor, the fishers talked of the abundance the river could yield if they worked as one, and the children dreamed of the stories they could create in the harmony of play.

Inspired by Aarav’s melodies, the villagers decided to bring their hearts and minds together. In the days that followed, they worked in unison. They sowed seeds not just in their fields, but in their souls, and as the crops grew, so did their bonds. The river Sarayu mirrored their unity, its waters nurturing the village like never before.

Years passed, and the village of Syama transformed into a haven of abundance and peace. The villagers learned that when they combined their intents and actions, they were not just a collection of individuals but a symphony of spirits dancing to the rhythm of life.

Aarav’s melody lingered in their hearts long after he had left, a reminder that in unity, there is well-being, in togetherness, there is strength. And so, the essence of the ancient Vedic verse lived on, not just in words but in the very breath of Syama.

Aarav’s Melody:

Together we sit, hearts entwined, intent as one,
In harmonious commune, our spirits begun.
For when minds align in a collective embrace,
Actions we take, flourish in grace.
In unity we find our purpose, our call,
In togetherness, we stand, lest we fall.
As identical hearts beat a rhythm so true,
Our united intentions in synergy brew.
So let us dwell in this bond, ever so tight,
In well-being together, our futures bright.
With every thought, every beat, every breath,
We affirm our union, from life unto death.
